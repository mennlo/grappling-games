# Sleeve Grip to Front Headlock or Single Leg

## Overview
An asymmetric standing drill where the attacker begins with two grips on the defender's sleeve (the "power hand") and works to transition one grip to the defender's collar, then uses push-pull combinations and foot-to-foot connections to off-balance the defender into a front headlock or single leg. The defender works to connect their own power hand to the attacker's collar or around their chest.

## Details
**Time:** 3 minutes, 2 partners or 6 minutes total - switching back and forth

### Attacking Player
**Position:** Standing facing your partner with both hands gripping one of the defender's sleeves (their "power hand")  
**Objective:** Maintain a same-side grip on the sleeve while securing a power hand grip on the defender's collar; use push-pull combinations with foot-to-foot connections to off-balance the defender and enter a front headlock or single leg  
**Win:** Defender is taken down due to an off-balance, OR attacker successfully enters a front headlock or single leg position

### Defending Player
**Position:** Standing facing your partner with one sleeve gripped by the attacker  
**Objective:** Prevent the attacker from securing a collar grip; connect your free power hand to the attacker's collar or around their chest  
**Win:** Successfully connect your power hand to the attacker's collar or around their chest

## Instructions
1. Attacker starts with both hands gripping one of the defender's sleeves
2. Attacker works to maintain a same-side sleeve grip while getting their other hand to the defender's collar
3. Once grips are established, attacker uses foot-to-foot connections and push-pull to create off-balance opportunities
4. Attacker uses the off-balance to enter a front headlock or single leg
5. Defender works to keep the attacker's free hand off the collar by connecting their own power hand to the attacker's collar or chest
6. Roles switch if: the defender is taken down, the attacker enters a front headlock or single leg, OR the defender connects their power hand to the attacker's collar or chest
7. Reset and repeat for the predetermined time

## Coaching Points
For the attacking player:
- Use your initial double sleeve grip to create tension — push and pull to break posture before entering the collar
- Enter the collar grip quickly by taking advantage of a moment of imbalance
- Use foot-to-foot connections (inside trips, outside trips) to compound your off-balancing
- Don't reach for the collar — create the opening first with the sleeve grip
- When the off-balance opportunity comes, commit fully to the headlock or single leg entry
- Chain your attacks: a failed headlock entry can become a single leg attempt

For the defending player:
- Your free hand is your primary weapon — get it to the attacker's collar or chest as early as possible
- Once your power hand connects to the attacker's collar, you control the tie-up and can set your own attack
- Use hip rotation to prevent the attacker's hand from reaching your collar
- Stay upright with good posture to limit their entry opportunities
- Stepping offline can disrupt their foot-to-foot connection attempts
- Use your feet actively to avoid being caught in a static off-balance
