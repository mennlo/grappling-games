# Headlock or Single Leg to Hip Cover

## Overview
An asymmetric standing drill where the attacker starts from a front headlock or single leg position and works to drive the defender to their hips and cover them, or connect to a rear bodylock and lift their partner or drive them down to their hands. The defender works to disconnect all connections and return to neutral standing.

## Details
**Time:** 3 minutes, 2 partners or 6 minutes total - switching back and forth

### Attacking Player
**Position:** Standing in a front headlock (chest covering partner's head) or single leg position (one of defender's legs controlled)  
**Objective:** Drive the defender to their hips and cover their hips, OR connect to a rear bodylock and lift your partner or drive them down to their hands  
**Win:** Defender's hips are covered, OR rear bodylock is connected and partner is lifted or driven down to their hands

### Defending Player
**Position:** Bent forward in a front headlock or with one leg controlled in a single leg  
**Objective:** Remove all connections and return to neutral standing  
**Win:** Successfully break all connections and return to a neutral standing position

## Instructions
1. Both players decide before the round which starting position to use — front headlock or single leg
2. Attacker starts with the position established; defender is in the corresponding defensive posture
3. Attacker works to drive or circle the defender down to their hips and cover them
4. Alternatively, the attacker can work to get behind the defender, connect a rear bodylock, and lift or drive them down to their hands
5. Defender works to break grips, create frames, and return to a neutral standing position
6. When the attacker achieves either win condition, switch roles
7. Reset and repeat for the predetermined time

## Coaching Points
For the attacking player:
- Stay heavy on the front headlock, keeping your chest low on the defender's upper back
- Use your head position to limit the defender's ability to create space and posture up
- Drive through the defender's resistance rather than trying to pull them
- When transitioning to the rear bodylock, use level changes and circular movement to get to their back
- Once the rear bodylock is connected, use lifting pressure and leg positioning to unsettle the defender's base
- In the single leg, keep your head on the outside, stay tight, and use your hips to finish

For the defending player:
- Create frames immediately to prevent being driven forward
- Focus on posture recovery first — get your head up before trying to escape the grips
- Use forward and lateral movement to prevent being circled or lifted from behind
- In the single leg, keep your base wide and use your free leg to post and stabilize
- Fight for underhooks to prevent the rear bodylock from being connected
- Stay active — static defense allows the attacker to set their weight
