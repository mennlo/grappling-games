# Dog Fight: Pull Guard or Pass

## Overview
A symmetric drill where both players start in a dog fight position — on their hands and knees, side by side, facing the same direction, one with an underhook and one with an overhook. Both players race to either pull guard with both legs between their partner's legs and a closed loop around their partner's upper body, or knock their partner over, get past their legs, and establish a closed loop around their partner's head and arm.

## Details
**Time:** 3 minutes, 2 partners or 6 minutes total - switching back and forth

**Position:** Both players on hands and knees, side by side, facing the same direction; one player has an underhook on their partner, the other has an overhook  
**Objective:** Pull guard with both legs in between your partner's legs and a closed loop around their upper body, OR knock your partner over, get past their legs, and establish a closed loop around their head and arm  
**Win:** Successfully pull guard with both legs in between your partner's legs and a closed loop around their upper body, OR knock your partner over, get past their legs, and establish a closed loop around their head and arm

## Instructions
1. Both players start on hands and knees, side by side, facing the same direction
2. One player has an underhook on their partner; the other has an overhook — alternate which player gets which connection each round
3. Both players simultaneously work toward either win condition
4. To win by pulling guard: get both legs in between your partner's legs and connect a closed loop around their upper body
5. To win by passing: knock your partner over, get past their legs, and connect a closed loop around their head and one arm
6. The first player to achieve either version of their win condition wins the round
7. Reset and repeat for the predetermined time

## Coaching Points
For both players:
- The underhook player has an advantage for pulling guard — use the underhook to pull your partner over you as you shoot your legs through to establish your guard
- The overhook player has an advantage for knocking the other player over — use your overhook to apply downward pressure and disrupt your partner's base
- The closed loop is critical on both paths — fight to create yours and fight to prevent your partner's
- Use your chest weight rather than just your arms to control the connection
- Stay low and connected — elevation gives the other player space to move
- The dog fight is a battle of who can dictate direction first — commit decisively when your opportunity opens
- After knocking your partner over, clear the legs quickly before they can establish a guard position
