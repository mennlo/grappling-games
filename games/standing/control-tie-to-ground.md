# Control Tie to Ground

## Overview
A competitive takedown drill where both players race to control each other in dominant positions. Starting from three different clinch positions, players work to get their opponent down to their hips or onto both hands, creating constant offensive and defensive pressure.

## Details
**Time:** 3 minutes, 2 partners or 6 minutes total - switching back and forth

**Position:** Standing with one of three clinch starting positions:
- Double inside bicep tie (both players controlling inside of partner's biceps)
- Russian two-on-one (one player controls one arm with both hands while other player has collar/neck control)
- Underhook with t-position (one player has underhook with pummel, other has arm frame or grip)

**Objective:** Get your opponent down to their hips or force both their hands to touch the mat  
**Win:** Successfully get opponent down to their hips in stable position, OR force both hands to mat simultaneously

## Instructions
1. Both players start in one of the three clinch positions
2. Both players simultaneously work to achieve the takedown goal
3. If starting from double inside bicep tie: both players work to drive opponent down
4. If starting from russian two-on-one: both players compete for dominance and takedown
5. If starting from underhook t-position: both players work their position advantage toward takedown
6. First player to achieve their win condition (opponent on hips or both hands down) wins the round
7. After a win, reset and START FROM A DIFFERENT POSITION than the one just used
8. Continue for full time period, rotating through the three starting positions
9. Switch roles completely after time expires

## Coaching Points
For both players:
- Capitalize on your current position - if you have the advantage, press it immediately
- Use your grips and ties to control your opponent's balance and movement
- Drive through your legs, not just your arms - power comes from your base
- Keep your posture and base strong to defend against opposing pressure
- React quickly to your opponent's direction and adjust your pressure accordingly
- Chain your attacks - if one takedown direction isn't working, shift to another
- Stay in constant motion - static positions are easier to defend
- Once you have control, move swiftly to complete the takedown
- Always have a plan for the next position after winning the current one
