# Asymmetric Touch to Bodylock

## Overview
An asymmetric standing drill where one player works to establish a bodylock or single leg takedown while the other player works to score touches behind the knee or force hands to the mat. This creates different strategic challenges for each role.

## Details
**Time:** 3 minutes, 2 partners or 6 minutes total - switching back and forth

### Bodylock/Single Leg Player
**Position:** Standing facing your partner  
**Objective:** Establish a bodylock (hands connected around partner's body) or secure a single leg takedown  
**Win:** Successfully lock your hands around your partner's body OR secure a single leg takedown  

### Touch Player
**Position:** Standing facing your partner  
**Objective:** Touch behind your partner's knee or get their hands to touch the mat  
**Win:** Successfully complete three touches (any combination of behind-knee touches or forcing hands to mat)

## Instructions
1. One player is designated as the bodylock/single leg player, the other as the touch player
2. Both players start standing, facing each other with no connections
3. Bodylock/single leg player works to either connect hands around partner's body (bodylock) or secure a single leg takedown
4. Touch player works to score three touches:
   - Touch behind partner's knee (either knee counts)
   - Force partner's hands to touch the mat (both hands must touch)
5. Each touch resets players to standing, but does not end the round
6. A successful single leg scores as a win just as a bodylock does
7. Round ends when either player achieves their win condition
8. After 3 minutes, switch roles and reset

## Coaching Points
For the bodylock/single leg/single leg player:
- Use level changes to close distance safely
- Set up your entries with collar ties or arm drags
- When you commit, commit fully to connection
- Anticipate snaps and keep a strong base
- Stay low to protect the knee touch — this also sets up single leg entries
- Use feints to draw out defensive reactions
- Chain bodylock attempts into single leg threats and vice versa
- When the touch player's hand position opens up, level change for the single leg
- Secure the single leg with proper head position and leg control

For the touch player:
- Maintain distance to create opportunities for touches
- Use snaps and pulls when the bodylock player enters
- Look for the knee touch when they level change
- Circle and angle away from their entries
- Don't let them get chest-to-chest
- Use push-pull combinations to off-balance
- Stay patient and let them make mistakes
