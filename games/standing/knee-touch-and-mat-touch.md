# Knee Touch and Mat Touch

## Overview
A competitive standing drill where both partners race to touch behind their opponent's knee or force their opponent's hands to touch the mat. First to achieve three touches wins.

## Details
**Time:** 3 minutes, 2 partners or 6 minutes total - switching back and forth

**Position:** Standing facing your partner with no initial connections  
**Objective:** Touch behind your partner's knee or get their hands to touch the mat  
**Win:** Successfully complete three touches (any combination of behind-knee touches or forcing hands to mat)

## Instructions
1. Both players start standing, facing each other with no connections
2. Both players simultaneously work to achieve three touches from these options:
   - Touch behind partner's knee (either knee counts)
   - Force partner's hands to touch the mat (both hands must touch)
3. Touches can be any combination (e.g., two knee touches and one mat touch)
4. Each successful touch resets players to standing
5. Keep count of touches throughout the round
6. First player to achieve three touches wins the round
7. If time expires before either player reaches three, player with more touches wins

## Coaching Points
For both players:
- Use level changes to set up your knee touches
- Use snaps and pulls to force your partner's hands to the mat
- Chain your attacks together - a knee touch attempt can set up a snap down
- Stay balanced with a good base to defend both attacks
- Keep your hands ready to post if snapped forward
- Use misdirection - look for one target, attack another
- Be ready to sprawl if your partner shoots low for the knee
- React quickly to your partner's level changes
- Maintain defensive posture while creating offensive opportunities
