# Butterfly Guard Closed Loop Control

## Overview
A drill focusing on establishing upper body control from butterfly guard while the top player works to split the legs or get outside position.

## Details
**Time:** 3 minutes, 2 partners or 6 minutes total - switching back and forth

### Bottom Player
**Position:** Seated in butterfly guard with both feet inside top player's thighs, leaning forward  
**Objective:** Establish a closed loop around the top player's upper body  
**Win:** Successfully create a closed loop around top player's upper body, either by locking hands behind their back or securing a seatbelt grip (one arm over the shoulder, one arm under the armpit) or over the shoulder with forearm across neck.

### Top Player
**Position:** Kneeling in front of bottom player  
**Objective:** Split the legs with one of your legs or get outside both legs and cover the hips of the bottom player  
**Constraints:** Must stay on your knees throughout the drill  
**Win:** Successfully split the legs with one of your legs or get outside both legs and cover bottom player's hips  

## Instructions
1. Bottom player starts seated in butterfly guard with both feet inside top player's thighs, leaning forward
2. Top player starts kneeling in front of bottom player
3. Bottom player works to establish a closed loop around top player's upper body
4. Top player works to split the legs or get outside both legs while staying on their knees
5. When either player achieves their win condition, switch roles
6. Reset and repeat until time expires

## Coaching Points
For bottom players:
- Maintain forward pressure with your chest
- Use your hooks actively to control distance and posture
- Work your grips systematically to create the closed loop
- Keep your feet engaged on the inside of their thighs
- Use head position to prevent them from creating space

For top players:
- Keep good posture on your knees to prevent being controlled
- Work to strip grips and create space
- Look for opportunities to post a knee between their legs
- Use your hands to control their hips and create angles
- Stay patient and systematic in your approach while staying on your knees
