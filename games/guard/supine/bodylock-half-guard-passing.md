# Bodylock Half Guard Passing

## Overview
An asymmetric drill where the top player uses a bodylock from half guard and works to complete the pass chest-to-chest with a closed loop (hands connected) around the defender's head and one arm.

## Details
**Time:** 3 minutes, 2 partners or 6 minutes total - switching back and forth

### Top Player
**Position:** On top in half guard with a bodylock (hands connected around the defender's torso)  
**Objective:** Pass the half guard and finish chest-to-chest with a closed loop around the defender's head and one arm  
**Win:** Achieve chest-to-chest contact with a closed loop (both hands connected) around the defender's head and one arm, having cleared the half guard

### Bottom Player
**Position:** In half guard with one of the top player's legs trapped between your legs  
**Objective:** Sweep the top player or reestablish a closed guard  
**Win:** Sweep the top player or reestablish closed guard

## Instructions
1. Top player starts in the half guard top position with a bodylock around the defender
2. Top player works to flatten the defender, clear the trapped leg, and advance to a chest-to-chest position
3. Top player must finish with a closed loop — both hands connected around the defender's head and one arm
4. Defender works to use their half guard to sweep or prevent the top player from advancing
5. Roles switch when the top player completes the pass with the closed loop, or when the defender sweeps or reestablishes closed guard
6. Reset and repeat for the predetermined time

## Coaching Points
For the top player:
- Use the bodylock to prevent the defender from turning away or building frames against your hips
- Apply cross-face or shoulder pressure to begin flattening the defender to their back
- Use your knee and hip movement to walk the trapped leg free while keeping the bodylock tight
- Once the trapped leg is cleared, advance your hips and transition the bodylock into a closed loop around the head and arm
- A tight closed loop around the head and arm prevents the defender from framing or turning back in
- Stay patient — clearing the half guard leg is the hardest part; don't rush the finish

For the bottom player:
- Use your top-side arm to frame against the top player's hip to prevent advancement
- Fight for an underhook to create space and turn into your opponent
- Use your trapped leg actively — push, angle, and reposition to off-balance the top player
- Attack with your knee shield or hip escape to prevent being flattened
- If the top player advances, urgently work to reestablish your knee shield or full guard before the closed loop is formed
