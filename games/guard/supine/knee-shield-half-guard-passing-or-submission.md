# Knee Shield Half Guard - Passing or Submission

## Overview
A drill starting from knee shield half guard where the top player can choose between passing options or direct submission attacks to win. This expands on standard passing options by allowing the top player to enter leg attacks or pursue other submissions as an alternative path to victory. Bottom player defends against both passing and submission threats while looking for guard recovery or sweeps.

## Details
**Time:** 3 minutes, 2 partners or 6 minutes total - switching back and forth

### Top Player
**Position:** In top half guard facing bottom player's knee shield  
**Objective:** Break down the knee shield and either complete a pass or finish a submission  
**Win:** Successfully complete the pass to side control or mount, OR tap the bottom player with any submission  

### Bottom Player
**Position:** In bottom half guard with knee shield established against top player  
**Objective:** Maintain knee shield, prevent the pass and any submission attempts, or create sweep opportunities  
**Win:** Establish closed guard, get both feet inside top player's legs and grab both ankles, or complete any sweep  

## Instructions
1. Top player starts in top half guard position facing bottom player's knee shield
2. Top player can pursue any passing option or shift to submission attacks at any time
3. For knee slice: drive knee across the thigh while maintaining chest pressure
4. For weave pass: get shoulder into the leg with knee smashed down, maintain weight on weave arm
5. For leg weave smash: pin both legs so knees point away, maintain weight on top leg
6. For leg attack entry: slide outside knee across belt line, underhook top leg, and turn to face the legs
7. Bottom player maintains knee shield and defensive frames while looking for sweep and guard recovery opportunities
8. When either player achieves their win condition, switch roles
9. Reset and repeat until time expires

## Coaching Points
For top players:
- Read the bottom player's reactions to decide whether to pass or enter a leg attack
- Leg attack entries are most available when the bottom player over-commits frames to stop the pass
- For knee slide to leg attack: cross the belt line, underhook the top leg, and face the legs
- Choke threats from front headlock are available if the bottom player turns away to avoid leg attacks
- Don't telegraph which route you're taking - use movement to force decisions
- The threat of submissions changes how the defender uses their frames - exploit the openings it creates

For bottom players:
- Stay aware of both passing and leg attack threats at all times
- Don't over-commit frames against pass threats if it opens the legs for an attack entry
- Protecting the knee line is critical when the top player slides toward a leg attack configuration
- If knee shield is broken, immediately work to square hips or insert hooks
- Maintain hip mobility to prevent being flattened or caught in an exposed position
- Use knee shield actively to create frames and prevent forward pressure from any direction
