# Closed Guard to Lasso Establishment

## Overview
A drill starting from closed guard where the guard player works to open and establish lasso guard while the top player works to disconnect both feet and move them to one side of their body.

## Details
**Time:** 3 minutes, 2 partners or 6 minutes total - switching back and forth

### Guard Player
**Position:** On your back in closed guard  
**Objective:** Open your closed guard, establish a lasso guard position, and use it to maintain guard or sweep your partner. If the top player stands up, you can attempt a double ankle grab sweep  
**Win:** Successfully establish/maintain lasso guard for the full duration or sweep your partner

### Top Player
**Position:** Inside your partner's closed guard, on your knees  
**Objective:** Get both of your opponent's feet disconnected from your body and positioned on one side of your body. You can stand up to work the pass  
**Win:** Successfully get both of your opponent's feet disconnected and on one side of your body


## Instructions
1. Start in closed guard with top player on their knees
2. Guard player opens their guard and works to establish lasso
3. Top player works to disconnect feet (can stand up)
4. Guard player attempts to thread leg around arm and secure sleeve grip
5. Once lasso is established, guard player works to maintain position or sweep
6. If top player stands, guard player can attempt double ankle grab sweep
7. Top player works to clear both legs to one side

## Coaching Points
For Guard Player:
- Open your guard with purpose and secure grips first
- Control distance with your feet as you work to establish the lasso
- Get the sleeve grip before threading your leg
- Use your other foot to maintain connection while establishing the lasso
- Keep your hips mobile to maintain favorable angles
For Top Player:
- Address grips early before the lasso is established
- Maintain good posture to avoid getting controlled
- Use knee pressure and hand fighting to prevent the lasso
- If they get the lasso, work immediately to clear it
- Move both legs to the same side to neutralize the guard
- Can stand up to create passing opportunities but be aware of ankle grab sweeps
- Stay balanced whether on knees or standing while working
- Maintain good posture to avoid getting controlled
- Use knee pressure and hand fighting to prevent the lasso
- If they get the lasso, work immediately to clear it
- Move both legs to the same side to neutralize the guard
- Stay balanced on your knees while working
