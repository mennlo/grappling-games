# HQ Pass to Submission or Pin

## Overview
A full sequence drill starting from HQ position where the top player works to complete the pass and finish with a submission or secure a dominant head-and-arm pin. The bottom player works to sweep, submit, or take the back from guard.

## Details
**Time:** 3 minutes, 2 partners - switching back and forth

### Top Player
**Position:** Standing in HQ position between the bottom player's legs  
**Objective:** Pass the guard and secure a submission. For new players, pinning the bottom player flat with a closed head-and-arm connection is an acceptable win  
**Win (Standard):** Complete the pass and finish any submission  
**Win (New Players):** Pin bottom player flat on their back with a closed connection around their head and far arm (hands clasped together) and hold for 3 seconds

### Bottom Player
**Position:** Lying on your back with one leg inside and one leg outside the top player's legs  
**Objective:** Prevent the pass and attack from guard  
**Win:** Any sweep to come on top, any submission, or take the back (both hooks in and seatbelt connected) held for 3 seconds

## Instructions
1. Top player starts standing in HQ position between the bottom player's legs
2. Top player works to pass the guard and secure a submission or pin
3. Bottom player works to sweep, submit, or take the back during the exchange
4. When either player achieves their win condition, switch roles and reset
5. Repeat for the duration of the round

## Coaching Points
For top players:
- Apply pressure from HQ first — flatten the bottom player before attempting to pass
- Look for submission opportunities during the pass (toehold, kneebar on the far leg) and immediately after (americana, kimura, arm triangle from side control or north-south)
- For the head-and-arm pin: wrap one arm under the bottom player's head and one arm over their near shoulder and under their far armpit, clasp your hands, and drive your chest weight into the pin
- Keep moving toward the pass — stalling in HQ without attacking gives the bottom player time to rebuild connections and frames

For bottom players:
- Maintain active inside connections with your feet and hands to create off-balance and sweep opportunities
- Watch for leg lock entries (outside heel hook, toehold) when the top player steps through to pass
- Look for back-take opportunities when the top player turns their back or ducks under during the pass
- Frame against the hip and head to slow the pass and create space to recover guard
- Stay proactive — purely defensive play allows the top player to settle their weight and pass methodically
