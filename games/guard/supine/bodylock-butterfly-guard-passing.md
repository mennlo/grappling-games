# Bodylock Butterfly Guard Passing

## Overview
An asymmetric drill where the top player uses a bodylock connection to pass butterfly guard, focusing on isolating the defender's legs using their legs and forearms/elbows to get past the guard. The top player does not need to achieve chest-to-chest contact — the goal is to cover the hips and get past the legs.

## Details
**Time:** 3 minutes, 2 partners or 6 minutes total - switching back and forth

### Top Player
**Position:** On your knees with the defender's feet on the inside of your thighs and a bodylock (hands connected around the defender's torso)  
**Objective:** Stay on top and pass the guard using the bodylock connection; if the bodylock grip is separated, work to reconnect it before passing; isolate the defender's legs using your legs and forearms/elbows  
**Win:** Get past both of the defender's legs and cover their hips

### Bottom Player
**Position:** Seated in butterfly guard with both feet on the inside of the top player's thighs  
**Objective:** Sweep the top player or prevent the pass  
**Win:** Sweep the top player or get to closed guard

## Instructions
1. Top player starts on their knees with a bodylock around the defender; defender's feet are between the top player's thighs
2. Top player uses their legs and forearms/elbows to isolate and neutralize the defender's legs
3. Top player focuses on getting past the legs and covering the hips — chest-to-chest contact is not required for this game
4. If the bodylock grip is broken, top player works to reconnect the grip before continuing the pass
5. Defender uses their butterfly hooks and upper body to create sweeping opportunities
6. Roles switch when the top player gets past both legs and covers the hips, or when the defender completes a sweep or gets to closed guard
7. Reset and repeat for the predetermined time

## Coaching Points
For the top player:
- Squeeze your knees together to limit the defender's ability to use their butterfly hooks
- Use your elbows and forearms to pin the defender's legs to the mat one at a time
- Stay heavy through the bodylock — use your chest weight to flatten the defender's upper body
- Prioritize reconnecting the grip immediately if it is broken — a loose bodylock is easy to sweep against
- Focus on getting one leg past at a time before completing the pass
- Use leg-stepping and hip movement to clear the defender's hooks without overcommitting

For the bottom player:
- Keep your feet actively engaged on the inside of the top player's thighs
- Use the bodylock against you — if the top player is tight, it can be difficult for them to create space to pass; if they are loose, it creates sweeping opportunities
- Attack with your butterfly hooks — use them to off-balance the top player and create angles for sweeps
- Use your upper body to create frames and prevent the top player from flattening you out
- If the top player loses the bodylock grip, aggressively attack to sweep or get to closed guard before they can reconnect it
- Stay patient — the top player will be focused on isolating your legs, so look for openings to attack when they are focused on their grip and leg control rather than the sweep defense
