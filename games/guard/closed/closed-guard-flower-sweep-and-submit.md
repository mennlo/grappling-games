# Closed Guard Flower Sweep and Submit

## Overview
A closed guard drill where the bottom player starts with one of the top player's arms trapped to their chest, working to complete a sweep, submission, or back take, while the top player works to escape the arm trap and pass the guard to side control.

## Details
**Time:** 3 minutes, 2 partners or 6 minutes total - switching back and forth

### Bottom Player
**Position:** On your back in closed guard with one of the top player's arms trapped to your chest  
**Objective:** Sweep, submit, or take the back from the arm trap position  
**Win:** Successfully complete a sweep, land a submission, or establish back control with both hooks in

### Top Player
**Position:** On your knees inside closed guard with one arm trapped to your partner's chest  
**Objective:** Escape the arm trap and pass the guard to side control  
**Win:** Successfully pass the guard and establish side control

## Instructions
1. Bottom player starts in closed guard with one of the top player's arms secured to their chest
2. Bottom player works to create the sweep angle, maintain the arm trap, and use the pendulum kick to complete the flower sweep
3. Bottom player can also look for submission opportunities (triangle, armbar, omoplata) or transition to back control if top player defends
4. Top player works to free their trapped arm and open the closed guard
5. Once the guard is open, top player drives to pass to side control
6. Reset when either player achieves their win condition
7. Switch roles after the predetermined time

## Coaching Points
For bottom player:
- Maintain tight control of the trapped arm by keeping it hugged to your chest
- Create the sweep angle by grabbing the leg/ankle and using a hip escape
- Use the pendulum kick to generate momentum and complete the sweep
- If the sweep is defended, look for the triangle, armbar, or omoplata before resetting
- Stay active and attack continuously — don't let the top player recover posture

For top player:
- Stay calm and work methodically to free the trapped arm
- Avoid posturing straight up, which exposes you to the pendulum kick
- Use hip movement and framing to create space and recover your arm
- Once your arm is free, immediately establish posture and work to open the guard
- Drive to pass aggressively once the guard opens — don't give the bottom player time to re-establish
