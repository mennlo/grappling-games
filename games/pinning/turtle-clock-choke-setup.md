# Turtle Clock Choke Setup

## Overview
A turtle position drill where the attacking player works to establish clock choke position (perpendicular to the bottom player with chest over back and collar grip), while the defending player in turtle position works to prevent the setup and escape.

## Details
**Time:** 3 minutes, 2 partners or 6 minutes total - switching back and forth

### Attacking Player
**Position:** Behind your partner who is in turtle position  
**Objective:** Establish chest-over-back contact, and secure a collar grip for the clock choke setup  
**Win:** Successfully establish position with chest over back and secure both collar grip with clear choke pressure  

### Defending Player
**Position:** In turtle position (elbows and knees) with your partner behind you  
**Objective:** Prevent perpendicular setup and collar grip acquisition while working to escape  
**Win:** Successfully turn to face your partner, stand up, sweep the top player over to become the top player

## Instructions
1. Defending player starts in turtle position (elbows and knees)
2. Attacking player starts behind the turtle with hands on the defender's back
3. Attacking player works to move to a perpendicular position while maintaining control
4. Attacking player establishes chest-over-back contact and works to grip the collar
5. Defending player works to prevent the perpendicular setup by maintaining body position and turning towards the attacker
6. Defending player can escape by turning to guard, standing up, or separating from the attacker
7. When either player achieves their win condition, reset and continue
8. Switch roles after the time period

## Coaching Points
For attacking players:
- Start with grip control on the back and hips to stabilize the turtle
- Circle to the side to begin perpendicular positioning
- Drive your chest over their back while moving around the side
- Keep your hips low and tight to maintain control during the rotation
- Secure the near collar grip first, then the far collar grip if possible
- Once positioned, maintain downward pressure with your chest
- Keep your head position over their back to prevent their escape
- Use your legs to block their attempts to turn into you
