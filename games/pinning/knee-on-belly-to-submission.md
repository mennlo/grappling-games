# Knee on Belly to Submission

## Overview
An advanced knee on belly drill where the top player works to isolate the bottom player's arms and secure submissions while staying outside the guard, while the bottom player focuses on escaping to guard or reversing position.

## Details
**Time:** 3 minutes, 2 partners or 6 minutes total - switching back and forth

### Top Player
**Position:** Knee on belly  
**Objective:** Isolate the bottom player's arms and submit them with any attack starting from knee on belly, staying outside of guard throughout  
**Win:** Successfully submit the bottom player with any submission starting from the knee on belly position  

**For newer players:** Work to isolate a head and arm connection (near-side head and far-side arm, or near-side arm pinned against body) while staying outside of the guard - submission is a bonus  

### Bottom Player
**Position:** On back with partner in knee on belly  
**Objective:** Escape to guard with leg control or reverse the position  
**Win:** Get back to guard with at least one of the top player's legs controlled above the knee, OR roll them over and become the top player  

## Instructions
1. Partners start with the top player in knee on belly position
2. Top player works to isolate the bottom player's arms and maintain knee on belly pressure while staying outside the guard
3. Top player can attempt any submission that starts from the knee on belly position
4. Top player must not allow their knee to fall inside the guard (between the bottom player's legs)
5. Bottom player works to escape by establishing guard with proper leg control or reversing position
6. Bottom player must control at least one leg above the knee (ankle traps don't count)
7. If bottom player achieves guard with leg control or becomes top player, they win the round
8. If top player submits bottom player, they win the round
9. Reset and continue for the full time period
10. After time expires, switch roles

## Coaching Points
For the top player:
- Keep your knee driving into the belly to maintain pressure and compliance
- Prioritize isolating the near-side arm by pinning it against the body with your shin
- Look for arm triangle (kimura, americano) setups from the near-side arm pin
- The far-side arm becomes available when the bottom player frames against your knee
- Stay on your feet and ready to post - avoid getting caught flat-footed
- If the bottom player turns away, look for the back take before they recover guard
- Beginners: focus on connecting head and arm (crossface + underhook side) before looking for submissions
- Don't let your posting foot get caught by the bottom player's legs

For the bottom player:
- Frame against the top player's ankle with your near-side elbow to prevent knee driving in
- Use your far-side hand to push on the top player's hip or knee
- Shrimp away and work to bring your near knee back to create a guard angle
- Time your escape for when the top player is reaching for your arm
- Look for opportunities to grab above the knee as you recover guard
- Don't give up your near-side arm - keep your elbows tight to your body
- Bridge toward the top player's posted foot to disrupt their base
- Once you have one leg above the knee, work to secure the second
