# Knee on Belly Transitions

**Time:** 3 minutes per partner (6 minutes total)

## Overview
A drill focused on maintaining knee on belly pressure and transitioning across the body, or escaping knee on belly back to guard or a reversal.

## Top Player
**Position:** Knee on belly on one side of your partner's body  
**Objective:** Maintain pressure and transition your knee on belly from one side of the body to the other and back  
**Win:** Complete a full transition - start on one side, move to the opposite side, and return to the starting side - OR take the back with both hooks and seatbelt for 3 seconds

## Bottom Player
**Position:** On your back with your partner's knee on your belly  
**Objective:** Break the knee on belly connection and recover guard or reverse the position  
**Win:** Get your legs back in between you and your partner (any guard besides quarter guard), OR reverse the position and come on top

## Instructions
1. Partners start with the top player in knee on belly position on one side
2. Top player works to slide their knee across and establish knee on belly on the opposite hip, then transition back to the original side
3. Bottom player uses frames, shrimping, and guard recovery tools to disrupt the top player's knee connection
4. If bottom player achieves their win condition, reset and switch roles
5. If top player completes the full transition sequence, reset and continue
6. Run for the full time period, then switch roles

## Coaching Points
For the top player:
- Keep your near-side knee driving into the belly - don't let it slip to the hip or thigh
- Use your far hand to post and maintain balance as you transition
- Control the near-side lapel or underhook during transitions to prevent scrambles
- When crossing to the opposite side, use a cross-knee slide or step-over
- Stay light on your feet and ready to post when the bottom player moves
- Keep your hips low and weight shifted into the knee as you transition
- Look for back-take opportunities when the bottom player turns into you

For the bottom player:
- Use your near-side elbow to frame against the top player's ankle or shin
- Shrimp away to create space before bringing your legs back in
- Time your escape to when the top player is mid-transition and less stable
- Use your outside arm to post and rotate onto your side
- Pull your near-side knee to your chest to create a guard recovery angle
- Consider bridging toward the top player's posted foot to disrupt their base
