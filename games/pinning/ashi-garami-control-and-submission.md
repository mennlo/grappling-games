# Ashi Garami Control and Submission

## Overview
A leg attack specialist drill starting from ashi garami position. The top player (in ashi garami) works to maintain control, improve the position, and work toward a finishing ankle lock submission. The bottom player (being attacked) works to free their legs and escape the positional trap by getting both legs inside their partner's or completely separating.

## Details
**Time:** 3 minutes, 2 partners or 6 minutes total - switching back and forth

### Top Player (Ashi Garami Position)
**Position:** In either butterfly ashi garami or regular ashi garami control - one leg between partner's legs, controlling their leg  
**Objective:** Maintain ashi garami position, continue developing control, and work toward ankle lock submission finish  
**Constraints:** Must maintain the ashi garami position throughout the attempt  
**Win:** Successfully land a clean ankle lock submission with tight control and proper mechanics, OR maintain position for 30+ seconds with clear progression toward submission

### Bottom Player (Being Attacked)
**Position:** Neutral leg, being controlled in ashi garami trap  
**Objective:** Escape the ashi garami position by getting both legs inside your partner's legs OR completely separate your legs entirely  
**Constraints:** Must achieve one complete leg freedom state  
**Win:** Successfully position both of your legs inside your partner's for defensive guard, OR get your legs completely free and separate from your partner

## Instructions
1. Top player starts in either butterfly or regular ashi garami position
2. Bottom player begins trapped in the position
3. Top player works to maintain position stability and control their partner's trapped leg
4. Top player works toward ankle lock submission while controlling the position
5. Bottom player immediately works to disrupt the position and escape
6. Bottom player can achieve win by: (a) Positioning both legs inside top player's legs, OR (b) Completely separating and freeing their legs
7. If bottom player achieves escape, reset and switch roles
8. If top player lands clean ankle lock submission, reset and switch roles
9. If time expires, assess progress: did top player have clear submission control or did bottom player nearly escape?
10. Continue switching for full time period

## Coaching Points
For top player (ashi garami):
- Secure your initial position with proper weight distribution
- Control the trapped leg with hand grips and body pressure
- In butterfly ashi: use your free leg to prevent escape angles
- In regular ashi: keep the trapped leg tight and use your upper body to control
- Work toward ankle lock systematically - don't rush the submission
- Create pressure progressively while maintaining position control
- Be aware of leg lock defense - control the knee and foot angles
- If they start escaping, re-establish position before attempting submission
- Use your upper body grips to prevent them from rolling out

For bottom player (defending):
- Don't panic - work methodically to disrupt the position
- Immediately establish frames to prevent pressure and submission setup
- Look to get your free leg active and create angles for escape
- Drive your hips and legs to create separation
- Work to get both legs inside their legs for defensive positioning
- If one escape path closes, shift quickly to the other
- Use your upper body to create space and prevent them from tightening submission
- Be aggressive with your escape attempts - passive defense loses the position fight
