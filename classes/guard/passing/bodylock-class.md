# Bodylock Class
**Class Length:** 1 hour

This class develops bodylock connections through a progressive series of drills spanning standing entries, guard passing, and ground control. Students will build skills from initial grip-fighting entries, through establishing headlocks and single legs, into bodylock guard passing and turtle control, finishing with a competitive dog fight drill that ties all the concepts together.

This class consists of several progressive drills that build upon each other. Each drill is available as an individual game file for reference.

1. [Headlock or Single Leg to Hip Cover](https://mennlo.github.io/grappling-games/md-viewer.html?file=games/standing/headlock-single-leg-to-hip-cover.md)
2. [Sleeve Grip to Front Headlock or Single Leg](https://mennlo.github.io/grappling-games/md-viewer.html?file=games/standing/sleeve-grip-to-headlock-or-single-leg.md)
3. [Bodylock Butterfly Guard Passing](https://mennlo.github.io/grappling-games/md-viewer.html?file=games/guard/supine/bodylock-butterfly-guard-passing.md)
4. [Bodylock Half Guard Passing](https://mennlo.github.io/grappling-games/md-viewer.html?file=games/guard/supine/bodylock-half-guard-passing.md)
5. [Turtle Defense and Hip Control](https://mennlo.github.io/grappling-games/md-viewer.html?file=games/pinning/turtle-defense-and-hip-control.md)
6. [Dog Fight: Pull Guard or Pass](https://mennlo.github.io/grappling-games/md-viewer.html?file=games/standing/dog-fight-pull-guard-or-pass.md)

**Total Drilling Time:** 36 minutes
- Standing Drills: 12 minutes
  - Headlock or Single Leg to Hip Cover: 6 minutes (2 x 3-minute rounds)
  - Sleeve Grip to Front Headlock or Single Leg: 6 minutes (2 x 3-minute rounds)
- Guard Passing Drills: 12 minutes
  - Bodylock Butterfly Guard Passing: 6 minutes (2 x 3-minute rounds)
  - Bodylock Half Guard Passing: 6 minutes (2 x 3-minute rounds)
- Ground Control Drills: 6 minutes
  - Turtle Defense and Hip Control: 6 minutes (2 x 3-minute rounds)
- Dog Fight Drills: 6 minutes
  - Dog Fight Pull Guard or Pass: 6 minutes (2 x 3-minute rounds)

## Standing Drills

### 1.1 - [Headlock or Single Leg to Hip Cover](https://mennlo.github.io/grappling-games/md-viewer.html?file=games/standing/headlock-single-leg-to-hip-cover.md)

**Overview:** An asymmetric drill where the attacker starts from a front headlock or single leg and works to drive the defender to their hips and cover them, or connect to a rear bodylock and lift or drive the defender to their hands. The defender works to disconnect and return to neutral standing.

**Time:** 3 minutes, 2 partners or 6 minutes total - switching back and forth

#### Attacking Player
**Position:** Standing in a front headlock or single leg position  
**Objective:** Drive the defender to their hips and cover their hips, OR connect to a rear bodylock and lift or drive them to their hands  
**Win:** Defender's hips are covered, OR rear bodylock is connected and partner is lifted or driven to their hands

#### Defending Player
**Position:** Bent forward in a front headlock or with one leg controlled in a single leg  
**Objective:** Remove all connections and return to neutral standing  
**Win:** Successfully break all connections and return to a neutral standing position

### 1.2 - [Sleeve Grip to Front Headlock or Single Leg](https://mennlo.github.io/grappling-games/md-viewer.html?file=games/standing/sleeve-grip-to-headlock-or-single-leg.md)

**Overview:** An asymmetric drill where the attacker starts with two grips on the defender's sleeve (their "power hand") and works to transition one grip to the collar, then uses push-pull combinations with foot-to-foot connections to off-balance the defender into a front headlock or single leg. The defender works to connect their own power hand to the attacker's collar or around their chest.

**Time:** 3 minutes, 2 partners or 6 minutes total - switching back and forth

#### Attacking Player
**Position:** Standing facing your partner with both hands gripping one of the defender's sleeves  
**Objective:** Maintain a same-side sleeve grip while securing a power hand grip on the defender's collar; use push-pull and foot-to-foot connections to off-balance the defender into a front headlock or single leg  
**Win:** Defender is taken down due to an off-balance, OR attacker enters a front headlock or single leg position

#### Defending Player
**Position:** Standing facing your partner with one sleeve gripped by the attacker  
**Objective:** Prevent the attacker from securing a collar grip; connect your power hand to the attacker's collar or around their chest  
**Win:** Successfully connect your power hand to the attacker's collar or around their chest

## Guard Passing Drills

### 2.1 - [Bodylock Butterfly Guard Passing](https://mennlo.github.io/grappling-games/md-viewer.html?file=games/guard/supine/bodylock-butterfly-guard-passing.md)

**Overview:** An asymmetric drill where the top player uses a bodylock connection to pass butterfly guard, focusing on isolating the defender's legs using their legs and forearms/elbows to get past the guard. The top player does not need chest-to-chest contact — the goal is to cover the hips and get past the legs.

**Time:** 3 minutes, 2 partners or 6 minutes total - switching back and forth

#### Top Player
**Position:** On your knees with the defender's feet on the inside of your thighs and a bodylock around the defender's torso  
**Objective:** Stay on top and pass the guard using the bodylock; isolate the defender's legs using your legs and forearms/elbows  
**Win:** Get past both of the defender's legs and cover their hips

#### Bottom Player
**Position:** Seated in butterfly guard with both feet on the inside of the top player's thighs  
**Objective:** Sweep the top player or prevent the pass  
**Win:** Sweep the top player or get to closed guard

### 2.2 - [Bodylock Half Guard Passing](https://mennlo.github.io/grappling-games/md-viewer.html?file=games/guard/supine/bodylock-half-guard-passing.md)

**Overview:** An asymmetric drill similar to 2.1, but starting from half guard and requiring a chest-to-chest finish with a closed loop around the defender's head and one arm.

**Time:** 3 minutes, 2 partners or 6 minutes total - switching back and forth

#### Top Player
**Position:** On top in half guard with a bodylock around the defender's torso  
**Objective:** Pass the half guard and finish chest-to-chest with a closed loop around the defender's head and one arm  
**Win:** Achieve chest-to-chest contact with a closed loop around the defender's head and one arm, having cleared the half guard

#### Bottom Player
**Position:** In half guard with one of the top player's legs trapped between your legs  
**Objective:** Sweep the top player or reestablish a closed guard  
**Win:** Sweep the top player or reestablish closed guard

## Ground Control Drills

### 3.1 - [Turtle Defense and Hip Control](https://mennlo.github.io/grappling-games/md-viewer.html?file=games/pinning/turtle-defense-and-hip-control.md)

**Overview:** A drill where the attacking player works to establish hip control and break down the turtle position, while the defending player works to prevent hip control and improve their position by turning to guard, standing up, or sweeping.

**Time:** 3 minutes, 2 partners or 6 minutes total - switching back and forth

#### Attacking Player
**Position:** Behind your partner who is in turtle position  
**Objective:** Establish hip control and work to break down the turtle position  
**Win:** Get both hooks in with seatbelt grip, or break down turtle to hip and establish chest-to-back contact with seatbelt grip

#### Defending Player
**Position:** In turtle position with your partner behind you  
**Objective:** Prevent hip control and improve your position  
**Win:** Successfully turn to guard, stand up, or sweep the top player over to become the top player

## Dog Fight Drills

### 4.1 - [Dog Fight: Pull Guard or Pass](https://mennlo.github.io/grappling-games/md-viewer.html?file=games/standing/dog-fight-pull-guard-or-pass.md)

**Overview:** A symmetric drill where both players start in a dog fight position — on their hands and knees, side by side, facing the same direction, one with an underhook and one with an overhook. Both race to either pull guard with both legs inside their partner's legs and a closed loop around their partner's upper body, or knock their partner over and establish a closed loop around their partner's head and arm.

**Time:** 3 minutes, 2 partners or 6 minutes total - switching back and forth

**Position:** Both players on hands and knees, side by side, facing the same direction; one player has an underhook, the other has an overhook  
**Objective:** Pull guard with both legs in between your partner's legs and a closed loop around their upper body, OR knock your partner over, get past their legs, and establish a closed loop around their head and arm  
**Win:** Successfully pull guard with both legs in between your partner's legs and a closed loop around their upper body, OR knock your partner over, get past their legs, and establish a closed loop around their head and arm

## Conclusion
Finish class with Q&A and some live rolling starting from a front headlock or single leg position. Focus on applying the bodylock connections learned during class, as well as practicing the closed loop finishing and hip control concepts when opportunities arise.
